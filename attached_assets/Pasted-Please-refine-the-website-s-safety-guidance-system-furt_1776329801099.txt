Examples of the type of precautionary guidance expected:

Role-based guidance:

For school authorities:
- consider delaying or adjusting morning assembly during severe heat, cold, heavy rain, or storm conditions
- reduce or suspend outdoor activities when environmental risk is high
- communicate safety alerts clearly to teachers and families
- arrange extra monitoring for vulnerable students
- keep school entry, exit, and movement areas safer during rain or flood-prone conditions

For teachers:
- monitor students for visible discomfort during extreme heat, cold, poor air quality, or rain
- reduce outdoor class activity when needed
- keep vulnerable or unwell students indoors when conditions are poor
- remind students about water, warm clothing, rain protection, or mask use where appropriate
- follow school safety instructions during severe weather

For guardians / parents:
- send children with water, warm clothing, rain protection, or masks as needed
- be careful about travel during heavy rain, flood-prone, or stormy conditions
- avoid unnecessary exposure for children who are unwell or environmentally sensitive
- follow school notices and weather-related safety updates

For students:
- carry water during hot conditions
- wear warm clothing during cold conditions
- use umbrella or rain protection during rain
- reduce outdoor exposure during poor air quality, storm, or severe weather
- follow teacher and school instructions carefully

Specific condition-based precautionary guidance:

For people with lung-related problems:
- reduce outdoor exposure during poor air quality, dust, or smoke-like conditions
- avoid strenuous outdoor activity when air quality risk is high
- keep necessary personal protective items or prescribed medical support if already advised by a doctor
- seek professional medical advice if breathing difficulty worsens

For people sensitive to cold:
- avoid prolonged exposure during cold mornings or cold-wave-like conditions
- wear sufficient warm clothing
- reduce early morning outdoor activity when cold risk is high

For people sensitive to heat:
- avoid prolonged exposure during hot periods
- reduce strenuous activity during high heat risk
- maintain hydration and stay in shaded or cooler places when possible

For people sensitive to rain or damp weather:
- avoid staying in wet clothes
- use rain protection
- be careful on slippery or waterlogged paths
- reduce unnecessary outdoor movement during heavy rain

For people with allergy problems related to heat, cold, or dust:
- reduce exposure to triggering conditions when risk is active
- avoid unnecessary outdoor exposure during dust or poor air quality
- use appropriate protective measures already recommended to them personally
- seek medical advice if symptoms become severe

For sick or vulnerable students:
- avoid prolonged outdoor exposure during high heat, cold, poor air quality, heavy rain, flood, or storm conditions
- remain under closer adult supervision when conditions are poor
- rest indoors when environmental conditions are unsafe
- follow professional medical advice if symptoms worsen

For sick or vulnerable teachers:
- reduce unnecessary outdoor exposure during adverse environmental conditions
- take added caution during travel and duty in severe weather
- follow professional medical guidance if symptoms worsen
